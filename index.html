My story

Feb. 2019After finishing my Ph.D I am doing a wrap-up postdoc working on several research projects in the areas of Bayesian modelling, parameter inference, optimal experiment design and stochastic systems

March. 2013 Started a Ph.D in Computational Systems Biology at CTSB Group, ETH Zurich. Here I developed numerical methods for the modelling of biological processes and for learning of the biochemical mechanisms from experimental data.
My work involved: C++, Matlab, Bayesian parameter inference, particle filters, various Monte Carlo methods, Dirichlet Processes.
April. 2018Moved to London to participate in a 4-month pre-idea and pre-team startup incubator.

Sept. 2010 Moved to Zurich for my M.Sc in Mathematics at the ETH Zurich, focusing on control theory and convex optimization. During my studies I deepened an practiced my coding knowledge during an internship as a Java programmer at Nebion Ag.

Sept. 2007 Moved to Munich for my B.Sc in Mathematics at the TUM, focusing on numerics and optimization. Simultaneously finished a Baccaulareat in Philosophy (equivalent to a B.Sc). While studying, I supported myself through a student job at an engineer consulting company Altran GmbH & Co.KG.

Sept. 2006 After finishing High School in Germany, I moved to Jerusalem where I was working as a volunteer with mentally and physically severly challenged children
